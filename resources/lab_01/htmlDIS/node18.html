Communication Primitives for Parallel Operating Systems:

We have long-term research cooperations with the German company Parsytec GmbH based on our expertise in parallel algorithms, the operation of parallel computers and the design of optimised parallel architectures. These successful efforts, partly supported by the ALCOM II project, represent a good basis for ALCOM-IT . The work in the field of process mapping will be expanded from the mapping of static process graphs to the mapping of dynamic process graphs and process migration. New algorithms will be designed, implemented and integrated in the next release of PARIX, the operating system of Parsytec. The ``virtual topology library'' integrated in the current PARIX releases has shown that such cooperation works very successfully.

    In our first project plan it was foreseen that the results of workpackage 4.1. will be exploited by Parsytec. At that time Parsytec had plans to integrate sophisticated methods for load balancing and distributed shared memory simulation into their operating system Parix. Due to the new market orientation of Parsytec, which now focusses on embedded parallel computing systems in vision applications, the company is no longer interested in extending the Parix operating system. Therefore Parsytec will not exploit the results of workpackage 4.1.
    Independently from our cooperation with Parsytec we have since about one year a dose cooperation with the Siemens Nixdorf Informationssysteme AG which is also located in Paderborn. This cooperation focusses on the joint development of a closely coupled workstation cluster using SCI- Interfaces provided by Dolphin Interconnect Solutions. The SCI cluster is assembled by SNI and the basic message passing interface is implemented by the University of Paderborn. The Daisy-library developed in workpackage 4.1 (Parallel Operating Systems) of the ALCOM-IT project integrates the simulation of shared memory and loadbalancing techniques for the efficient use of parallel and distributed systems. Using the special features of the SCI communication architecture (global address space and one-sided communication) it will be possible to implement these services very efficiently on the SCI cluster. The adaptation of Daisy to the cluster system, which Will be done in 1998, will lead to a system which aids the exploitation of the enhanced computation and communication capabilities of SCI technologies.

We shall also provide algorithmic strategies for the realisation of global variables in distributed memory machines. Here we combine the advantages of hashing and caching strategies: hashing reduces congestion, i.e., ensures that no hot spots occur, caching tries to maintain data locality. Some of these methods have been already successfully implemented in a library of basic services, and integrated in OCCAM-light, an extension of the parallel language OCCAM. The development of these parallel algorithms will be supported by performance modeling. A sufficiently detailed abstract model of the parallel architecture will be specified. The model provides the implemented algorithms with their architecture dependent parameters. This work will also support the design of new parallel architectures.
