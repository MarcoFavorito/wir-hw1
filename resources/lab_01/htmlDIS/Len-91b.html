Careful closure of inheritance networks

Maurizio Lenzerini

Inheritance Hierarchies in Knowledge Representation and Programming Languages

The careful closed world assumption allows one to apply the closed world reasoning to a specified set of (not necessarily all) predicates of the knowledge base. In this paper we consider a subclass of inheritance networks, namely those inheritance networks which can be expressed as a set of Horn clauses, and study the complexity of reasoning about such theories under the careful closed world assumption.
