InfoPath 2003

Summary

InfoPath 2003, the new information-gathering program of the Microsoft Office System of products, makes it easier to collect information and reuse it throughout an organization. Companies today typically collect and use massive amounts of information in forms, spreadsheets, or e-mail. But because the data is trapped within documents or databases scattered throughout the organization, key decision makers often don't have the correct data to make the most informed decisions. InfoPath helps address these problems. Download this document to learn more about InfoPath.

Live Communications Server 2003 Standard Edition

Live Communications Server 2003 is an enterprise real-time communications server that provides an extensible platform to deliver presence capabilities and instant messaging to connect people, information, and business processes so that employees can make better decisions faster. Live Communications Server 2003 provides enterprise grade security and manageability, is built on a standards-based extensible platform used by thousands of developers, and is integrated into everyday programs and processes—all unified through a familiar user interface (UI).

Connect People, Information, and Processes in Real Time

Enable employees to communicate and make better decisions faster by integrating presence and communications capabilities into programs and processes.

  • Incorporate instant messaging into individual productivity programs, collaborative programs, and e-mail programs.
  • Enhance communications and collaboration tools with real-time capabilities.
  • Integrate presence information, instant messaging, and real-time communications capabilities into line-of-business applications, including customer relationship management (CRM), enterprise resource planning (ERP), and alerting systems.
  • Enhance team collaboration by enabling team members to see which other team members are online and contact them immediately with presence capabilities and instant messaging integrated into programs and portal Web sites. Coworkers working together on a specific task don't need to be on each other's contact list.
  • Enable employees to work together without the constraints of geography, office locations, time zones, or networks. Employees can instantly work with other through instant messaging.
  • Enable employees to use instant messaging to communicate during conference phone calls, allowing for coaching and parallel discussions.
