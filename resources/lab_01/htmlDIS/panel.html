A user's perspective on distributed simulation

Overview: Describe, from a user's perspective, the hard and/or interesting aspects of applying distributed simulation for analysis, acquisition or training. Provide insights with respect to areas where research can make life easier for the user of distributed simulation.

Motivation: The PADS community is largely a collection of academic researchers who have been involved primarily with algorithms/techniques for efficient synchronization of parallel/distributed discrete event simulation processes. These are the hardcore Time Warp and Chandy-Misra-Byant folks. The synchronization problem, however, seems largely to have been solved. Many of the recent workshops have been populated mostly with papers that are "fine-tuning" existing approaches, e.g. pushing known synchronization algorithms down into network interface cards. While applications of their synchronization algorithms are flourishing (e.g. telecommunications) these papers are presented at applications-oriented conferences (e.g. MASCOTS). PADS remains dedicated to the "core" technology/algorithm issues. As the synchronization problem becomes well-solved, the existence of PADS as annual gathering of learned researchers is threatened. Our objective in organizing the panel is to attempt to identify some challenging "core" distributed simulation technology issues that may offer the PADS research community a "life after synchronization".
