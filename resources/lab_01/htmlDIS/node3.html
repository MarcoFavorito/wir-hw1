Project Objectives

  Computing speed is a key factor in a society increasingly pervaded by the use of computers in all aspects of life. Efficient data structures and algorithms are needed to design and manage complex systems, such as railways, factories, production-planning systems, distributed networks that support a wide variety of users and services through heterogeneous technologies, multimedia information systems, and parallel computers with thousands of processing elements to solve heavy computational problems. The need for efficiency becomes more acute as systems and computers become larger and more complex, and as the effective use of resources in existing systems becomes more important. In order to meet the challenges posed by such systems, ALCOM-IT aims to bridge the gap between research in the field of algorithms and applications in information technology, and to play for European industry the role that the strong algorithm groups within IBM Research and Bell Labs play for their companies. (It should be noted that even large European companies do not have comparable groups in their research departments.) In particular, we shall

  • deliver correct, efficient, and easy-to-use libraries of combinatorial software of general applicability and embodying the best algorithms and data structures,

  • provide solutions to efficiency-critical algorithmic and optimisation problems arising in particular IT application domains, e.g., management of large computer and communication networks, advanced graphical software engineering environments, logistics, and resource management,

  • transfer to application developers, in industry and elsewhere, new paradigms, methodologies, tools for algorithm design and analysis and best-practice criteria,

  • develop new algorithms and new methods for their analysis and design, and improve the understanding of efficient computing.

All of the goals above are currently being addressed by the ongoing ALCOM research effort, which we plan to continue with a better focus and with greater emphasis on the applied part of our research. Our proposed research splits into applied and basic research, which are intimately connected in our approach: the applied research produces results of immediate utility outside ALCOM-IT , while the basic research yields fundamental insights and lays the foundations for future applied research. Our work will be organised along three major streams: software libraries, foundations and methods, and technology transfer to the Information Technology industry.
