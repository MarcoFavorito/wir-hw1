Biographic sketch

At a glance

Camil Demetrescu graduated in Computer Science in 1997 at the University of Rome "La Sapienza". He received a Ph.D. in Computer Science in February 2001 from the same University. He has been visiting scientist at the AT&T Research Laboratories (Florham Park, NJ, USA), joining the Networks Group, and at the Theory Group of the IT University of Copenhagen. He has been consultant at Microsoft Research - Silicon Valley. He is currently Assistant Professor in Computer Science at the University of Rome "La Sapienza". His research interests include the design, analysis, and engineering of algorithms for combinatorial problems. His scientific results include both theoretical and applied contributions. He has solved long-standing open problems on graphs and has designed and implemented several software systems for algorithm engineering and visualization. His results appeared in prestigious venues such as Journal of the ACM, IEEE Symposium on Foundations of Computer Science, and ACM Symposium on Theory of Computing. His was recipient of the 2001 EATCS Italian Chapter Award for the best two Ph.D. Theses in Theoretical Computer Science. He has organized several scientific events, including the 45th IEEE Symposium on Foundations of Computer Science. He is continuously invited to join the program committees of international conferences and has been recently co-chair of the 2005 ACM-SIAM Workshop on Algorithm Engineering and Experiments.
