LEDA:

We shall further develop and improve the LEDA library aided by feedback from our users. Such feedback is well established through electronic mail and a LEDA news group and has led to significant improvements already (e.g., a twofold increase in the speed of the LEDA graph data type). Our main emphasis will be placed on improved information dissemination, reliability, dynamic graph algorithms and approximation algorithms for NP-complete problems. On the theoretical side we plan to work on a theory of constant factors; this theory should allow one to predict the actual running time of actual programs on actual machines within a factor of two. A significant part of LEDA is now so mature that, on the one hand, several companies have made it the basis of serious software development, and, on the other hand, it is no longer a scientific challenge to maintain it. The recently founded LEDA GmbH distributes and maintains these parts of LEDA and develops MS-DOS and Windows versions of it. We shall keep close ties with this company and act as consultants for it.
