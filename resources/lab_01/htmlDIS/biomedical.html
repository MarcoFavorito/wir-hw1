Modeling and Analysis of Biomedical Systems

Research activities

The research activity in this scientific area lies, at present, in two main projects: analysis and modelling of metabolic systems and motor control.

In the context of the first project mathematical models were applied to the study of the metabolism, with particular regard to body composition, tracer kinetics, pharmacokinetics, and assessment of effects of drugs and nutrients that stimulate b-cell function. In particular, model-based methods were developed  for the analysis of the kinetics of dicarboxylic acids to be used as an alternative energy substrate,  and for the assessment of body composition using several techniques as BIA, DXA, TC imaging and NMR

With regard to motor control, the research activity was concerned with the analysis and processing of EEG and event related potentials, recorded on the scalp, in order to perform different physiological and clinical studies, such as modeling of brain sources, Alzheimer disease analysis, development of Brain-Computer Interfaces. In this context, the interest was focused on the methods for the pre-processing, processing and classification of EEG changes in power spectra, during tasks concerning the imagination of a  movement  Methods based on a modelling approach were also developed, in order to investigate on the functional connectivity of the cortical sources.  The research activity in this scientific area lies, at present, in two main projects: analysis and modeling of metabolic systems; motor control.
