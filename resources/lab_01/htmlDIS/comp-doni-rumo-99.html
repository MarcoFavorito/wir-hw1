Engineering of KR-Based Support Systems for Conceptual Modelling and Analysis

Ernersto Compatangelo, Francesco M. Donini, and Gianni Rumolo

Information Modelling and Knowledge Bases X

Most of present intelligent knowledge management environments for conceptual modelling and analysis suffer, in our opinion, from mixing two representation levels: (1) a conceptual level, where domain-specific concepts are represented (e.g. data and processes in Data-Flow Diagrams); (2) an epistemological level, where inferences are drawn from domain-independent linguistic constructors (e.g. concepts and roles in Description Logics). We propose an engineering approach to the development of new systems, where the two levels are separately represented and are either linked by the protodl methodology or by concept emulators. We exemplify our approach by modelling Data-Flow Diagrams in level (1) and by using a Description Logic with a Closed Terminology Assumption in level (2). The benefit of the proposed approach lies in decoupling the conceptual level from the underlying epistemological level in the overall architecture of support tools for the construction and the analysis of large domain models. This implies easy replacement of different inferential engines at the epistemological level, while retaining the same domain description at the conceptual level.
