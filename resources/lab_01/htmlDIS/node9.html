Synergies:

The three tasks outlined above will be coordinated by Saarbrücken, Rome, and Cologne respectively. They will closely interact with each other and will produce a coherent whole. We give three examples of interactions within the project. Saarbrücken is already using a prototype of ABACUS for an algorithm for the maximum planar subgraph problem which in turn is a key ingredient for the GDToolkit. ABACUS will use existing algorithms in LEDA for cut generation, and GDToolkit uses the planarity testing algorithm of LEDA among others. Users outside the project will experience similar synergy.
