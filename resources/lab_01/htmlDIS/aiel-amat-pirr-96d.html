Intuitionistic Autoepistemic Logic

Gianni Amati, Luigia Carlucci Aiello, and Fiora Pirri

Studia Logica

In this paper we propose intuitionistic logic as the minimal logic most suitable to define autoepistemic logic. We start from a formal analysis of the notion of consistency on an intuitionistic basis, via Gentzen's sequent calculus. Then we show that the epistemic operator M interpreted as a consistency operator behaves coherently and is also an adequate interpretation of the intuitionistic consistency notion. Intuitionistic stable sets are introduced and given a semantics, on the basis of a bimodal structure, which is shown to be sound and complete. The results of the work are related to Gabbay's ~-models. The relation between our formalization and other works on autoepistemic logic is also investigated.
