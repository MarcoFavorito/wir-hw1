Classic Planning for Mobile Robots

Giuseppe De Giacomo, Luca Iocchi, Daniele Nardi, and Riccardo Rosati

Proceedings of the FAPR-96 Workshop on Planning in Complex Environments

The paper describes an approach to planning within the framework of description logics. Our proposal is "classic" since it is in the style of deductive planning and relies upon the reasoning capabilities of description logics, that are realized in the system Classic. The outcomes of our work are twofold. From an epistemological point of view our approach is based on the formalization of actions given by dynamic logics. We take advantage of both the representational mechanisms provided by description logics and the introspective capabilities obtained by extending the language with an epistemic operator. From a practical viewpoint we show how a general purpose knowledge-based environment, based on description logics, is used in order to implement an actual agent, the mobile robot "Tino", whose reasoning tools allow it to derive plans from the knowledge about the environment and the action specification.
