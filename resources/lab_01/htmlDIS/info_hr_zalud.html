Our experience with visual telepresence applied to two robotic systems (U.T.A.R. and Orpheus) will be presented. A brief technical description of both robotic systems as well as the concept of mixed operator-based control with semi-autonomous mode will be provided to underline the necessity of advanced and intuitive remote control of the robot for the success in reconnaissance missions. The advantages of the digital video data processing over analog one will be presented. We will show the five-years evolution of our user interface with main emphasize to the newest version made under DirectX with digital data in the form of semitransparent displays. Our idea of controlling all of the robot functions through advanced joystick system (H.O.T.A.S. - Hands on Throttle and Stick) will be also described.

The lecture will be accompanied by Orpheus robotic system demonstration.

Discussion will form essential part of the lecture.
