Digital 3D models of the environment are needed in rescue and inspection robotics, facility managements and architecture. This lecture presents an automatic system for gaging, digitalization and interpreting of 3D indoor environments. It consists of a high speed autonomous/teleoperated mobile robot, a reliable 3D laser range finder and elaborated software modules.

Kurt3D is the fastest indoor robot of the world. It's based on a mobile robot platform with a size of 45 cm (length) x 33 cm (width) x 26cm (height) and a weight of 15.6 kg. Equipped with the AiS 3D laser range finder the height increases to 47 cm and weight increases to 22.6 kg. Kurt3D's maximum velocity is 5.2 m/s (autonomously controlled 4.0 m/s. Designing a control program for autonomous mobile robots means the implementation of a software program, i.e., several cascaded controllers or behaviors including set value computation. The lecture presents breifly the control of Kurt3D as well as a novel, laser-based approach for tracking the pose. Reliable laser tracking in high speed mobile robotics has to cope with sensor noise, e.g., vibrations.

Mobile robots equipped with a 3D laser range finder are well suited for gaging 3D data. Elaborated algorithms allow to digitize large environments fast and reliably without any intervention and solve the simultaneous localization and mapping (SLAM) problem. Robot motion on natural surfaces has to cope with yaw, pitch and roll angles, turning pose estimation as well as scan matching or registration into a problem in six mathematical dimensions, i.e., 6D SLAM. The exploration of the environment is done by a Next Best View planner that computes the next nominal pose by maximizing the information gain.
