History

 

 

Events 

   In February 1998 Consorzio Padova Ricerche approved the project presented by Prof. Daniele Nardi (Univ. of Rome "La Sapienza") for relizing a team of soccer robots to participate at the scientific competition organized by the RoboCup Federation in the "Middle-size" category.
   The project was joined by other italian universities (Brescia, Genova, Milano Politecnico, Palermo, Parma) thus forming ART (Azzurra Robot Team) the Italian national team of soccer robots coordinated by Daniele Nardi.
  ART has proposed the first model of national team and furthermore has organized for the students of the participating universities a School for the Design of Soccer Robots in Padua (June 1998).
   During this school the students have learned basic notions for realizing and programming mobile robots.
   ART-98 has participated at:

  • Robocup-98 (Paris, France, July 2-9)

Further information on ART-98 are on http://www.dis.uniroma1.it/~ART/ and in the technical paper "Azzurra Robot Team".

Participants

Daniele Nardi, Luca Iocchi, Luca De Nubila, GianPaolo Pucci, Stefano Dell'Accio, Stefano Armenia, Claudio Castelpietra, Stefano Squillaci, Marcello Velati from University of Roma "La Sapienza", and 5 professors and 20 students from universities of Brescia, Genova, Padova, Palermo, Parma, and Politecnico di Milano.

Results of ART at RoboCup-98

The competition included 16 teams from USA, Japan, Australia, Germany, France, Italy and Portugal. ART was at the second place in the preliminary round, winning against then team from Munchen (Germany), drawing with Melbourne (Australia) and Tubingen (Germany). ART has played quarter finals against the team of Uttori (Japan), drawing 0-0 after the regular time, drawing the penalty kicks 1-1, and being defeated in the last challenge.
