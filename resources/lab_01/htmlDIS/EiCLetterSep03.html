Letter from the Editor-in-Chief

 

Dear Colleagues,

From October 2003, submissions to the IEEE Transactions on Robotics (T-RO) are open. The first issue of T-RO will appear in August 2004.

This new journal of the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society stems from the IEEE Transactions on Robotics and Automation (T-RA), which will end publication with the issue of June 2004. During the last few years, T-RA became the leading archival journal in Robotics and Automation. In 2003, T-RA is heading toward a number of submissions of over 400 papers for the third year in a row; the full electronic submission and review processes and the dedication of the Editorial Board have brought to submission-to-decision review times of 150 days on the average; the acceptance rate is just below 30%, and T-RA has had the highest impact factor in the field for many years.

It  will be our primary goal to keep these excellent records for T-RO as well. The legacy of T-RO which will be inherited from T-RA is evidenced by several formal and substantial facts. T-RO will have the same Editorial Board of T-RA, as it stands at the end of September 2003. The composition of the Board will then evolve gradually to its Robotics focus. T-RO shall continue with the volume/issue numbering and bimonthly schedule of T-RA, keeping also the same cover appearance. Since the percentage of robotics-related papers that have appeared in T-RA is around 75% of the total, at steady state T-RO will have enough space to further expand its coverage, both in the traditional areas and in new or emerging topics of interest to Robotics.

T-RO shall publish scientific papers on all aspects involving robots and intelligent machines and systems, from fundamental theory of interdisciplinary nature to innovative applications for industry, for remote and unstructured environments, and for human assistance and service. In addition, our efforts will be devoted to address issues of Robotics science “and beyond,” exciting borderline or strategic subjects that may become essential in the long run.

T-RO is looking forward to receiving fine contributions by authors from all over the world, with the goal of publishing papers that disseminate science and technical advances in Robotics for a better life.

Alessandro De Luca
T-RO Editor-in-Chief
Dipartimento di Informatica e Sistemistica
Università di Roma “La Sapienza”
Roma, Italy
