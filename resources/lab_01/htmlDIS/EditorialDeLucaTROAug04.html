Editorial

 

Welcome to the IEEE Transactions on Robotics (T-RO). With the publication of this August issue, the first with a new name, the transition process started by the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society (RAS) in February 2003 is complete. RAS has now fully replaced its top-cited IEEE Transactions on Robotics and Automation (T-RA) with two parallel Transactions, the IEEE Transactions on Automation Science and Engineering (T-ASE), whose first issue has just appeared in July, and the present one.

 

T-RO is not a brand new journal, as it starts with Issue 4 of Volume 20 in the bimonthly T-RA series. This entitled us to keep the same ISBN number and, most importantly, the same citation index record of T-RA ---not by chance, as the large majority of papers that have appeared in T-RA were in Robotics. On the other hand, the updated “single name” of our Transactions will result in an expanded coverage of the area. In order to ensure this, the Editorial Board has been smoothly changed, with about the half of the new Associate Editors and one new Editor with respect to one year ago.

 

During the last two decades, Robotics has emerged as a multidisciplinary field, yet with its own methodologies, algorithms, and technologies. Expanding from the original, well-defined, and mainly manufacturing-oriented applications, Robotics has become the key science to address complex and autonomous motion, sensory, and cognitive tasks in unstructured environments. Robots are getting closer to the long-expected humanoid form, they are able to explore the remote space, to sense and act at a micro- and nano-scale, to learn from experience, to assist surgeons, to serve as companions or human helpers. We should also not forget advances in more traditional performance indices, such as speed, precision, payload-to-weight ratio, and reliability. T-RO shall reflect this variety of contexts and continue to present solid, creative, and exciting results and realizations of robotics research.

 

Indeed, this may be accomplished only through high-quality papers coming from the broadest scientific community interested in Robotics. Since the opening in October 2003 until the middle of this July, T-RO has received about 300 new submissions, i.e., slightly more than one-paper-a-day. Although we are still publishing papers originally sent to T-RA (and will continue to do so within the current year), I am glad to report that this issue contains already two papers (one regular and one short) that were new submissions to T-RO. I emphasize that this is also a good time for presenting proposals for Special Issues. Please, check the T-RO website at http://www.dis.uniroma1.it/ieeetro or contact me for further information and procedures.

 

Some final editorial information. In an effort to reduce the time to dissemination of results, T-RO started participating to the rapid posting program of IEEE Xplore. After acceptance and galley proof correction, the pdf file of a paper is immediately posted in an “accepted for future publication” section of the new T-RO IEEE Xplore page (see http://ieeexplore.ieee.org/xpl/RecentIssue.jsp?puNumber=8860), ready to be consulted, downloaded, and referenced even before the hard copy version is issued.

Moreover, T-RO is currently evaluating the move to the latest version of Manuscript Central by ScholarOne, a centralized and integrated paper handling web system that is being used by over 70 IEEE Transactions. This step will be finalized in 2005. Although our internal electronic submission and web-based paper review system has been successfully in use for about 5 years, two major improvements are expected from this change: the streamlining of the whole process from original manuscript submission to final publication, with direct output to IEEE, and the possibility of handling multimedia attachments from the submission stage. With on-line publications becoming a standard, T-RO will thus take full advantage of IEEE Xplore features such as “extended objects” attached to each paper (this may include your robotics videos), full-text search capabilities, and cross-reference linking.
