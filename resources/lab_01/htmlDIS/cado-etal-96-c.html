Experimental Analysis of the Computational Cost of Satisfiability Checking in Logics for Commonsense Reasoning

Marco Cadoli, Andrea Giovanardi, Enrico Giunchiglia, Fausto Giunchiglia, Marco Schaerf, and Roberto Sebastiani

Technical Report, Dipartimento di Informatica, Sistemistica e Telematica, Università di Genova

Many reasoning tasks in logics for commonsense reasoning have been shown to be computationally intractable in the worst case. However, However, realistic applications of KR require the ability to handle large knowledge bases, which require efficient and reliable algorithms. A first step in the development of algorithms which are efficient in the average case is to single out those cases which appear in practice to require more computational resources. The goal of our investigation is to perform experimental studies on sophisticated KR formalisms, similar to those performed for classical logic. In particular, in this paper we analyze modal and non-monotonic logics.
