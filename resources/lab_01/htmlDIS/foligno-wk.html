International Workshop

Planning and real-time monitoring of rescue operations

Palazzo Trinci, Foligno

25 October 2002

 

Motivation

The project "Real-time planning and monitoring for search and rescue operations in large-scale disasters" is being developed within the framework of the program "Agenzia 2000" of the Italian National Research Council (CNR), with the goal to study methods and techniques for planning and information acquisition, with specific application to the emergency scenarios that may arise after earthquakes. The project is carried out by Univ. Roma "La Sapienza", Politecnico Milano, Univ. di Genova and Dir. Gen. Protezione Civile e Servizi Antincendio and has selected as a case study for experimentation the city of Foligno and the data concerning the earthquake of Marche and Umbria 1997.

 

Aim

The goal of the Workshop is to provide a forum for discussion and exchange of ideas among researchers aiming at the design and implementation of tools to support search and rescue operations after catastrophic events like earthquakes, floods, or other disruptive actions taking place in urban environments. As shown in the literature, the technologies that can be applied in the design of tools for this purpose are manyfold, spanning through several disciplines: from the studies of analysis and previsional models, that are carried out within computer science, operation research, system science to those concerning the environment, territory, buildings and other structures. Specifically, the aim of the workshop is to address rescue operations in scenarios where information on the situation are incomplete and dynamically updated. Therefore, the workshop is specifically focussed on two issues that are critical in emergency management:

  • planning and execution monitoring
  • situation assessment

Moreover, special attention is given to the design and deployment of integrated tools to support analysis and management of information, as well as simulation and planning of actions.
