Local Search Techniques for High School Timetabling

Andrea Schaerf and Marco Schaerf

ICPTAT-95

The school timetabling problem regards the weekly scheduling for all the classes of a high school. The problem consists in assigning lectures to periods in such a way that no teacher or class is involved in more than one lecture at a time. This problem has been proved NP-complete and is usually tackled using heuristic methods. This paper reports an on going experimentation with local search techniques (e.g. Simulated Annealing and Tabu Search) for school timetabling. Our experiments give a comparative analysis of the effectiveness of the various techniques for this specific problem. In addition, we explore some issues related to the type of move allowed for the navigation of the search space and we discuss which is the best structure for the objective function so as to make the search capabilities of the technique more effective.
