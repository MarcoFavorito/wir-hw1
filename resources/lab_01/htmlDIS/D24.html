Models for Communication Networks

 

Deliverable 24:

Modeling the Internet Graph



Short Description: At variance with the proposed original title of the deliverable (multi-view models of the Internet graph), we have preferred to use a more generic definition. Indeed, the models we present here are intrinsically global (hence multi-view). Rather, it is the data analysis part that suffers from the single- vs. multi-view issue: as paper [9] shows, single-view data can be distorted with respect to the real ones.

This is a collection of papers where the problem of a realistic description and modelisation of the Internet graph is attempted. First of all, it is important to stress that modeling the topology of a network such as the Internet is intrinsically intertwined with the analysis of real data, since real data suggest properties that some models can capture and some others cannot. Internet modeling is also related to the study of the dynamics of the social systems that actually use the Internet as a tool. Indeed the Internet evolves according to the needs of its users, and to a host of mechanisms (market driven, governement regulated and so on). As such, the simplest rules that have been hystorically associated to Internet models (such as preferential attachment) have to be seen as an effective way to capture some social interactions between Internet actors. These considerations clearly point to a connection of this Deliverable D24 with D20 (Statistical Properties of Collected Data) and WP4 (Dynamics of Social Networks).

Looking at the bare topology is quite a primitive way to look at the Internet, and the focus is slowly shifting to weighted networks, where nodes and edges other than being present or absent, are also characterized by their relevance in the network.

For a more detailed description of the work done in these directions, read the second year report (in .doc or .pdf format). Bibliographical citations refer to the list of papers below.
