Software Libraries for Parallel and Distributed Computing

The current state of parallel and distributed computing is similar to the state of sequential computing before the advent of LEDA and similar platforms: a host of theoretically efficient algorithms is known, but actually implementing these algorithms is exceedingly time-consuming and error-prone because of the lack of software packages for even the most basic tasks. The situation is considerably more complicated than in the sequential setting due to the many radically different brands of parallel machines, and because the younger field of parallel and distributed computing has had less time to achieve a consensus on terminology and methodology. We shall initiate the development of a library for basic algorithms and data structures for distributed memory machines and shall continue our work on distributed systems simulation.
