Most Specific Concepts Technique for Knowledge Bases with Incomplete Information

Francesco M. Donini and Angelo Era

Proceedings of the International Conference on Information and Knowledge Management (CIKM'92)

We evaluate the technique of most specific concepts in knowledge bases when dealing with incomplete information. The two forms of incomplete information we consider are existential quantification and disjunction. The languages we use to express the knowledge base are concept languages, a well studied family of languages stemmed from KL-ONE. The result of our analysis is that most specific concepts are still useful when dealing with existential quantification, while they are no more convenient in presence of disjunction. The result is significant since present knowledge base systems, such as BACK, do not use this technique when dealing with indefinite knowledge.
