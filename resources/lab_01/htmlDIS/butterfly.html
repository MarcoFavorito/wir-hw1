Control of Underactuated Robots

The Butterfly Robot 

 

The Butterfly is a particular kind of underactuated robot, consisting of a single motor driving the link shown in the picture above. As the name indicates, it is shaped like a butterfly, and a ball can freely roll on its edge. There are many interesting control problems that can be studied with this system; one of them, which we are currently addressing, is illustrated in the figure below:
  • The objective is to execute a 180 degrees rotation of the link without losing contact with the ball, and to stabilize the ball position on the opposite side of the Butterfly.
  • To this end, the contour of the link has been studied in order to avoid singularities, and a polynomial profile has been chosen
  • We are currently studying the possibility of including a visual feedback to improve the robustness of the controller

 

If you want to have an idea of the difficulty of the proposed task you can click here and have some fun watching us while we try to do it manually!

The Butterfly robot was originally conceived and studied by Kevin Lynch of Northwestern University, Illinois. Take a look at his pages for more information: http://lims.mech.nwu.edu/~lynch/papers/index.html. In particular, you can download and read the following paper:

K. M. Lynch, N. Shiroma, H. Arai, and K. Tanie. The roles of shape and motion in dynamic manipulation: The butterfly example. 1998 IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation, pp. 927-932, Leuven, Belgium, May 1998

Our version of the Butterfly has been designed and realized by the Ph.D. students Massimo Cefalo (cefalo@dis.uniroma1.it) and Fabio Zonfrilli (zonfrilli@dis.uniroma1.it).
