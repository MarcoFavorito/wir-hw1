Minimal models in propositional logics: approximation results

Abstract

In this work we define optimization problems concerning different notions of models. In particular, we analyze the different settings of two, three and four-valued propositional logic: in each setting we focus on the problem of minimizing the number of literals belonging to a model or maximizing the number of literals that do not belong to a model of a CNF formula. First, we formulate these problems as the optimization versions of well-known model-finding problems in Artificial Intelligence, concerning the areas of nonmonotonic reasoning and abductive reasoning. Then, we characterize these problems in terms of complexity approximation classes. We state, from one side, negative results concerning non-approximability in polynomial time for the general problems, and find, from the other side, syntactical restrictions that allow for approximate solutions.
