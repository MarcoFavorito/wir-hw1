Proceedings (Published by ACM Press)

The Proceedings volume contains the papers presented at the Advanced Visual Interfaces Workshop, AVI '94 held in Bari at Villa Romanazzi on June 1-4, 1994. As in the previous workshop (held in 1992 at the University of Rome) the main goal was to enable a working discussion about the main issues related to the evolution (or revolution?) of visual interfaces - namely - visualization strategies, window management, spatial syntax and semantics, computational metaphors, multimedia exploitation, usability metrics, visual programming.

Along these lines we have invited a selected number of distinguished speakers who gave us their personal views on most of the above issues. During the first day, Ben Shneiderman spoke on both the notion, and the corresponding application, of dynamic queries, Aaron Marcus gave us his views on the use of metaphors in visual interfaces with particular emphasis on the graphical layout. As for the other invited speakers, David Harel spoke on sub-optimal algorithms for improving the aesthetics of graphs on a screen and Eric J. Golin on the problems and on some possible solutions, for the design of visual programming environments; as the last invited speaker, Jim Foley gave a full coverage of all the exciting projects which are presently running at the Graphics, Visualization & Usability Center at the Georgia Institute of Technology in Atlanta.

Two panels were organized: the first one on the different features that should be incorporated in the future visual interfaces as, for instance, utilities for navigating in wide, complex information spaces, comfortable interaction for a wide number of user classes, easy-to-learn functions even on palm-top computers, application of recently discovered usability features and clever integration of multimedia support. The second panel, on European research in the area of visual interfaces discussed the current projects, their inter-relationships, the possible connections to current work done in other countries.

We received more than fifty papers; among them 17 have been selected as full papers, and 5 as short papers, not because of a lower quality, but because they were somehow marginal to the workshop theme. The papers which were selected (about 50% of the submitted ones) ranged from new features incorporated in prototype interfaces to visualization of complex data, virtual reality, interactive scenarios in a number of present and foreseen applications. A significant part of the workshop was dedicated to the subject of visual querying which is steadily growing in breadth and depth (application domains and theoretical work) since the techniques which are being developed are proving effective in allowing a wide spectrum of users to profitably obtain the required data from a number of different database systems.

Lastly, a certain number of working prototypes have been also selected and demonstrated during all the days of the workshop, they definitely helped in understanding the ideas of the developers, the difficulties found in the corresponding implementation and in detecting critical points and collecting suggestions from the participants.

The content of this volume is organized in nine chapters. The first chapter contains the contributions of the invited speakers. Chapters from 2 to 6 include the papers presented at the corresponding technical sessions of the workshop. Chapter 7 contains the position statements of the two panels. Finally, Chapter 8 and 9 include the short papers and the description of the presented systems respectively.

We are indebted with the program committee members and the referees for their precious work. Special thanks are due to Tiziana Toni for her valuable help in solving all the technical problems related with the system demos. Finally, we deeply acknowledge the generous contributors who made this workshop possible.
