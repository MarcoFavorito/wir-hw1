Ongoing research projects

QUIS (QUality, Interoperability and Standards in e-learning): eLearning Programme, 2004-3538/001-001 ELE-ELEB14

QUIS is a project aimed at topics in open and distance learning, granted by the European Commision under E-Learning programme. There are five distinguished partners, joining from four different European countries: TISIP (Norway), NTNU (Norway), University "La Sapienza" (Italy), MSU (Sweden), SZAMALK (Ungary). QUIS activities began Jan 2005.

mENU: models for a European Networked University eLEARNING initiative: project No 2002-0510/001-001-EDU-ELEARN.

The MENU project sets out to create a model for a European Networked (Virtual) University. The model will include an organisational structure, a quality assurance system, examples of joint courses and study programmes across institutional borders, guidelines and a demonstrator of a practical e-learning environment. The demonstrator will focus on ICT-related studies.

The project expired December 31st, 2003, and, as a matter of fact, we had quite good acceptance by the eu commission:

  • mENU representatives were at the eu stand to present mENU in the LearnTec (Karlsruhe, DE, Feb 2004);
  • Then a full page (!) has been dedicated to mENU in the EU publication Better eLearning for Europe;
  • Then the jury of the European eLearning Award eureleA invited mENU to participate in that contest. Then one page mENU description appeared in The Parliament Magazine, Issue 192, 15th November 2004.
